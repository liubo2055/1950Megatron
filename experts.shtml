Rationale

--an explanation of controlling principles, opinions, and beliefs of our web design & development

Design

We are redesigning in a neat way and focusing only on ease of content. We believe the lecture notes are the most important part of the website, so we've strove to keep the tutorial information as clear as possible. Also, we add a back-to-top button and both site and page navigation helping the student to find course information in a simple and fast manner.

Funtionality

All funtionality are related to the lecture notes, such as resource links and code examples on each page for students to further seek out information related to class topics. We also add an interactive disqus comment area for feedback of lecture notes.

We've intentionally decided to discard the use of fancy images, multimedia content, and anything other than what helps to achieve the goal of the site: to deliver students the best course information.

News

A tumblr blog was incorporated as a way for students to stay updated about current news and announcements of the class.

Audience

We believe that the audience should be very specific but not general. So we make the most of every space of the site to provide particular information only towards students of the ongoing class.

Marketing

The COMP1950 website is primarily for COMP1950 class. So we've foregone any attempt to devise plans for marketing.

Compatibility

The design of the website uses HTML5 and CSS3. We've ensured that each page and line of code has been validated by the WC3. And the site will render well on mobile phones, tablets, etc, as we did anticipate that these platforms would be used by the audience regularly to access information. The navigation menu was redesigned to the top right on mobile phones for better user experience. Wireframes for desktop, tablet and mobile are all provided.
